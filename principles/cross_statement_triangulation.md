# Cross Statement Triangulation

## What The Principle Means

Cross-statement triangulation tests whether the income statement, balance sheet, cash-flow statement, notes, and commentary tell a coherent story. A red flag becomes more important when several statements point in the same direction.

## Why It Matters In Quarterly Reporting

Quarterly disclosures can be thin. A company may explain a movement in narrative form but omit the schedule that would confirm it. Triangulation helps the reviewer use available evidence without overstating it.

## How The Issue Can Affect Reported Performance

Income can rise through accruals before cash arrives. Cash flow can improve through working-capital timing before profit improves. Balance-sheet accounts can reveal the future cost of current-period profit, such as rising provisions, deferred costs, or customer credits.

## Where It May Appear In The Financial Statements

- Income statement trends.
- Balance-sheet account movements.
- Operating cash-flow working-capital lines.
- Notes to revenue, receivables, inventory, provisions, debt, and segments.
- Management commentary.
- Adjusted metric reconciliations.

## How To Triangulate It Across Statements

Start with a claim or observed trend. Identify the related balance-sheet account, cash-flow line, note disclosure, and management explanation. Test whether all sources support the same interpretation. If they conflict, treat the conflict as the finding.

## Typical Red-Flag Patterns

- Revenue growth, rising receivables, weak cash collection, and limited customer-balance disclosure.
- Margin expansion, rising capitalized costs, and falling expense ratios.
- Adjusted EBITDA growth, recurring exclusions, and flat operating cash flow.
- Reduced liabilities, lower expense, and no clear settlement or release explanation.
- Improved working capital from one-off actions while demand indicators weaken.

## Possible Benign Explanations

- Different statements capture different timing.
- A large customer paid after quarter-end.
- A new contract structure changed balance-sheet geography.
- Inventory was built for confirmed demand.
- A liability was settled after negotiation.

## Questions A Reviewer Should Ask

- What evidence outside the income statement confirms the result?
- Which statement disagrees with management's narrative?
- Is the inconsistency temporary or recurring?
- What note would normally explain the movement?
- What subsequent-period data would clarify the signal?

## Limitations Of The Analysis

Triangulation depends on the quality of available disclosures. Missing schedules can produce false positives or leave important risks unobserved.

## How The Codex Skill Should Use The Principle

Use triangulation as the default evidence standard. A scanner should escalate concern when multiple statements support the same signal and lower confidence when the signal appears in only one metric.
