# Public Company Review Limitations

## What The Principle Means

A public-disclosure review is limited to what the company has filed, released, or presented. It can identify inconsistencies, missing explanations, unusual trends, and questions for management, but it cannot resolve facts that require internal records or audit evidence.

## Why It Matters In Quarterly Reporting

Quarterly filings can be unaudited and less detailed than annual filings. Companies may not provide full reserve rollforwards, contract detail, customer aging, covenant calculations, or post-period collections. The reviewer must communicate uncertainty clearly.

## How The Issue Can Affect Reported Performance

Incomplete disclosure can make normal movements look concerning or hide important risks. A strong or weak signal may change once annual filings, footnotes, audit procedures, or management responses become available.

## Where It May Appear In The Financial Statements

- Condensed interim notes.
- Management discussion.
- Earnings releases.
- Investor presentations.
- Segment tables.
- Non-GAAP reconciliations.
- Risk factors and subsequent-event disclosures.

## How To Triangulate It Across Statements

Use every available public document, but do not fill gaps with unsupported assumptions. If the quarterly filing lacks detail, compare with the latest annual report, prior quarters, earnings call transcript, and investor presentation when supplied.

## Typical Red-Flag Patterns

- Material movements with no note discussion.
- Changed definitions without a reconciliation.
- Missing prior-period comparatives.
- Cash-flow statement absent from an earnings release.
- Adjusted metrics disclosed more prominently than GAAP results.
- Segment changes that reduce comparability.

## Possible Benign Explanations

- Interim reporting rules require less detail.
- The item is immaterial under company policy.
- A schedule appears only annually.
- A business model change required new metrics.
- The company disclosed more detail in a separate document not supplied.

## Questions A Reviewer Should Ask

- Which missing document would most improve the analysis?
- Is the same disclosure available in the annual report?
- Did prior quarters include more detail?
- Has management explained the omission elsewhere?
- Should confidence be lowered because of missing evidence?

## Limitations Of The Analysis

The skill cannot audit, subpoena, inspect contracts, verify customer existence, or determine compliance with accounting standards. It should remain a structured reading aid.

## How The Codex Skill Should Use The Principle

Every output should include a limitations section. When data is missing, the skill should identify the specific missing evidence rather than weakening the entire analysis with vague caution.
