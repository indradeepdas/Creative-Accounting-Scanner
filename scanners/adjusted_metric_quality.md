# Adjusted Metric Quality Scanner

## Purpose

Review adjusted earnings, adjusted EBITDA, free cash flow, constant-currency metrics, segment measures, and other supplemental metrics for consistency, completeness, and decision usefulness.

## Core Principle

Adjusted metrics can clarify performance when they isolate transitory items. They can obscure performance when they remove normal costs, ignore cash outflows, change definitions opportunistically, or receive more prominence than comparable GAAP measures.

## When to Use

Use whenever an earnings release, presentation, or filing includes non-GAAP, adjusted, organic, pro forma, constant-currency, EBITDA, free cash flow, or segment-adjusted measures.

## Required Inputs

- Adjusted metric definitions.
- Reconciliation to the nearest GAAP measure.
- Prior-period definitions.
- Cash-flow statement.
- Notes on restructuring, stock-based compensation, acquisition costs, impairment, tax items, and other exclusions.

## Financial Statement Areas to Inspect

- Non-GAAP reconciliation tables.
- Management commentary.
- Income statement.
- Cash-flow statement.
- Segment disclosures.
- Debt covenant metric definitions when disclosed.

## Key Red-Flag Patterns

- Definitions change without clear reason.
- Recurring cash costs are excluded every quarter.
- Adjustments remove unfavorable items but retain favorable similar items.
- Adjusted metrics improve while GAAP income or operating cash flow deteriorates.
- Free cash flow excludes capitalized recurring costs.
- Adjusted EBITDA is described as a cash-flow substitute without enough qualification.

## Suggested Calculations

- Total adjustments as a percentage of GAAP net income or operating income.
- Recurring adjustments over the last four quarters.
- Adjusted EBITDA to operating cash flow.
- Adjusted net income to GAAP net income.
- Free cash flow after capitalized software, contract costs, and lease payments where disclosed.
- Impact of each adjustment on EPS.

## Cross-Statement Triangulation

Tie every adjustment to the income statement, cash-flow statement, and notes. Compare management-adjusted performance with cash-backed performance and reviewer-normalized observations.

## Possible Benign Explanations

- A genuinely unusual charge occurred.
- Acquisition or divestiture costs are time-limited.
- Constant-currency metrics help isolate operating growth.
- Covenant metrics follow contractual definitions.
- Management reconciles changes transparently.

## Escalation Logic

Escalate when adjusted metrics drive the positive story but GAAP results, cash flow, or recurring cost trends do not support it. Escalate to High when definitions change and the change materially improves the metric.

## Evidence Standard

Require the exact reconciliation and definition. If reconciliation is missing, treat the output as limited and avoid calculating unsupported adjusted metrics.

## Management Questions

- Why are the excluded costs not part of normal operations?
- How often have similar adjustments occurred?
- Did definitions change from prior periods?
- What is the cash effect of each adjustment?
- How should users reconcile adjusted EBITDA to operating cash flow?

## Output Format

Provide an adjusted metric quality table with metric, GAAP comparator, adjustments, recurring status, cash effect, definition consistency, and concern level.
