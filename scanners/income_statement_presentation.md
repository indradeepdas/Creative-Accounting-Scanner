# Income Statement Presentation Scanner

## Purpose

Evaluate whether income-statement classification, labels, subtotals, and line-item changes make recurring operating performance easier or harder to understand.

## Core Principle

Presentation can affect perception even when net income is unchanged. Earnings quality is clearer when recurring and transitory items are separated consistently and explained.

## When to Use

Use for every quarterly filing or release, especially when operating income, gross margin, adjusted earnings, restructuring charges, gains, discontinued operations, or line-item changes are prominent.

## Required Inputs

- Current and prior income statements.
- Notes describing line-item composition.
- Non-GAAP reconciliations.
- Segment disclosures.
- Management commentary on margins and operating income.

## Financial Statement Areas to Inspect

- Revenue and cost of revenue.
- Gross profit.
- Operating expenses.
- Operating income.
- Other income and expense.
- Discontinued operations.
- Restructuring, impairment, gains, and special items.
- Segment profit measures.

## Key Red-Flag Patterns

- Operating income includes gains or benefits that do not appear operational.
- Costs move between cost of revenue and operating expense, altering gross margin.
- Similar costs are labeled differently across periods.
- Recurring charges are repeatedly described as unusual.
- Other income or below-the-line items are material to EPS.
- Segment profit definitions change without reconciliation.

## Suggested Calculations

- Gross margin before and after known reclassifications.
- Operating margin excluding disclosed gains or recurring charges.
- Other income as a percentage of pre-tax income.
- Restructuring and impairment charges over multiple quarters.
- Segment profit bridge to consolidated operating income.
- EPS contribution from non-operating or discrete items.

## Cross-Statement Triangulation

Compare presentation changes with notes, adjusted metrics, segment commentary, and cash-flow effects. Rebuild a simple recurring operating view when enough detail exists.

## Possible Benign Explanations

- Business model changed.
- Accounting standard or policy changed.
- Segment structure changed.
- A real exit or disposal occurred.
- Management improved disclosure granularity.

## Escalation Logic

Escalate when classification materially changes trend interpretation and the company does not provide enough detail for comparison. Escalate further when presentation benefits align with other weak signals.

## Evidence Standard

Require a measurable effect on subtotal interpretation or comparability. A different label is not enough without trend impact or missing reconciliation.

## Management Questions

- Did any line-item definition change this quarter?
- What would gross margin and operating margin look like under prior-period presentation?
- Which gains, charges, or expenses are recurring?
- How do segment measures reconcile to consolidated results?
- Why is the selected subtotal useful for evaluating operations?

## Output Format

Provide a presentation bridge showing reported subtotal, identified classification items, normalized subtotal where possible, disclosure quality, and management questions.
