# Liability Completeness Scanner

## Purpose

Assess whether liabilities, accruals, provisions, deferred revenue, refund obligations, warranties, contingencies, and debt-related obligations appear complete relative to disclosed risks and operating activity.

## Core Principle

Liabilities represent present or future claims on resources. Earnings quality weakens when expenses appear low because obligations are under-accrued, released without explanation, or omitted from prominent metrics.

## When to Use

Use when margins improve, accruals decline, provisions change, contingencies are disclosed, deferred revenue is material, customer refunds or warranties exist, or covenant and liquidity pressure is present.

## Required Inputs

- Current and prior balance sheets.
- Accrued expenses and other liabilities.
- Provision, warranty, restructuring, lease, debt, and contingency notes.
- Income-statement expense detail.
- Cash-flow statement.

## Financial Statement Areas to Inspect

- Accrued expenses and other current liabilities.
- Deferred revenue and contract liabilities.
- Warranty, return, rebate, and refund obligations.
- Restructuring provisions.
- Litigation and regulatory contingencies.
- Lease and debt obligations.
- Tax liabilities.

## Key Red-Flag Patterns

- Accrued expenses decline while revenue, headcount, or operating activity grows.
- Provision releases boost earnings without clear settlement or reduced exposure.
- Deferred revenue declines while customer obligations appear ongoing.
- Warranty or return liabilities fall while sales of covered products rise.
- Contingency language worsens without recorded accrual or range disclosure.
- Covenant definitions exclude obligations that remain economically important.

## Suggested Calculations

- Accrued expenses as a percentage of operating expenses.
- Warranty liability as a percentage of covered product revenue.
- Deferred revenue growth versus subscription or service revenue growth.
- Provision releases as a percentage of operating income.
- Debt and lease obligations versus EBITDA and operating cash flow.
- Current liabilities growth versus revenue growth.

## Cross-Statement Triangulation

Compare expense levels with liability balances and cash payments. If expenses fall because provisions are released, check whether cash settlements, risk reduction, or plan completion supports the release. Compare deferred revenue with future performance obligations and revenue growth.

## Possible Benign Explanations

- Obligations were settled in cash.
- A restructuring plan ended.
- Product quality improved.
- Customer terms changed.
- Legal exposure decreased.
- Seasonality affected accrual timing.
- A contract mix shift reduced advance billings.

## Escalation Logic

Escalate when liabilities decline and earnings benefit without a clear operating or cash explanation. Escalate to High when declining obligations conflict with worsening risk disclosures or rising business activity.

## Evidence Standard

Require a link between liability movement and expense, cash payment, or disclosed risk. Do not assume incompleteness from low liabilities alone.

## Management Questions

- What drove the liability or provision movement?
- How much of the change affected current-period earnings?
- Were obligations settled, remeasured, or reclassified?
- What exposure remains after the period-end balance?
- How does the liability compare with activity levels and historical claims?

## Output Format

Provide a liability completeness table with beginning balance, expense or provision, cash paid, releases, ending balance, earnings effect, and questions.
