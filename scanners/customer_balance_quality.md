# Customer Balance Quality Scanner

## Purpose

Review receivables, contract assets, unbilled revenue, customer advances, deferred revenue, return assets, and allowance accounts for signs that customer balances are becoming less collectible, less transparent, or less aligned with revenue.

## Core Principle

Customer balances connect reported revenue to future cash collection or future performance obligations. Deterioration in these balances can indicate that reported revenue is less cash-backed or that future revenue has been pulled forward.

## When to Use

Use when revenue growth is meaningful, cash conversion is weak, receivables are material, contract assets are present, or the company sells through distributors, resellers, long-term contracts, subscriptions, or milestone arrangements.

## Required Inputs

- Gross and net receivables.
- Allowance for credit losses or doubtful accounts.
- Contract assets and liabilities.
- Deferred revenue.
- Revenue and cash-flow statement.
- Aging, concentration, or credit-risk disclosures where available.

## Financial Statement Areas to Inspect

- Accounts receivable.
- Unbilled receivables and contract assets.
- Allowance accounts and bad debt expense.
- Deferred revenue and customer deposits.
- Sales return liabilities and refund obligations.
- Credit-risk and customer-concentration notes.

## Key Red-Flag Patterns

- Receivables or contract assets grow materially faster than revenue.
- Allowance ratio declines while receivables age, customer risk rises, or macro conditions worsen.
- Bad debt expense falls despite rising customer balances.
- Deferred revenue declines despite stated bookings or demand strength.
- Customer concentration rises without collection or credit discussion.
- Large balance reclassifications reduce comparability.

## Suggested Calculations

- Receivables as a percentage of trailing revenue.
- DSO using quarterly and trailing revenue.
- Allowance as a percentage of gross receivables.
- Bad debt expense as a percentage of average receivables.
- Contract assets as a percentage of revenue.
- Deferred revenue growth versus revenue growth.
- Customer deposits as a percentage of future revenue indicators.

## Cross-Statement Triangulation

Tie receivables and contract assets to revenue growth and operating cash flow. Tie deferred revenue to subscription, maintenance, backlog, or performance-obligation commentary. Compare allowance movement with credit-risk language and macro exposure.

## Possible Benign Explanations

- Normal annual billing patterns.
- Higher mix of large customers with longer payment terms.
- Public-sector collection timing.
- New milestone contracts.
- Billing-system migration.
- Acquisition of a business with different customer terms.

## Escalation Logic

Escalate when customer balances expand faster than revenue and operating cash flow weakens. Escalate further when allowance ratios fall, customer risk rises, or disclosures fail to explain the movement.

## Evidence Standard

Require a period comparison. A high receivable balance is not enough; show deterioration, unexplained movement, or inconsistency with cash flow or disclosures.

## Management Questions

- What portion of receivables is current versus past due?
- What changed in payment terms or customer mix?
- What collections occurred after quarter-end?
- Why did the allowance ratio change?
- What explains contract asset growth and expected billing timing?

## Output Format

Present a customer-balance bridge with revenue, receivables, contract assets, deferred revenue, allowance ratio, cash-flow support, and open questions.
