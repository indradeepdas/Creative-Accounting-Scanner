# Uncertainty Rules

## Confidence Scale

- 0% to 30%: Weak signal or insufficient data.
- 31% to 60%: Plausible signal with meaningful missing evidence.
- 61% to 80%: Supported by multiple data points but still subject to benign explanations.
- 81% to 100%: Strong document-level support, with major alternative explanations addressed.

## Lower Confidence When

- The period is seasonal.
- The company completed acquisitions or divestitures.
- Foreign exchange significantly affected results.
- Only an earnings release is available.
- Cash-flow data is missing.
- Notes are summarized or absent.
- The company changed accounting policies or segment definitions.

## Raise Confidence When

- The same pattern appears across several periods.
- Balance sheet and cash-flow evidence agree.
- Disclosures omit expected explanations for material movements.
- Adjusted metrics remove recurring costs or recurring cash outflows.
- Management commentary conflicts with statement data.
