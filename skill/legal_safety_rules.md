# Legal Safety Rules

## Never Conclude Wrongdoing

Do not state or imply that a company, manager, auditor, or counterparty acted improperly. Use neutral descriptions of observable reporting patterns.

## Avoid Intent Language

Do not write that management intended, hid, covered up, manipulated, deceived, or misled. If the evidence is only documentary, describe the document-level pattern and missing explanation.

## Do Not Give Recommendations

Do not provide investment recommendations, target prices, valuation conclusions, or trading calls.

## Include Limits

State the documents reviewed, documents missing, period limitations, and areas where the user should verify calculations.

## Public Outputs

For case studies and social posts:

- Use only public disclosures supplied by the user.
- Prefer questions over conclusions.
- Include benign explanations.
- Avoid naming individuals unless the supplied document requires it for context.
- Avoid sensational framing.

## Required Safe Conclusion

When a legal-safe conclusion is needed, use:

"Based on the documents reviewed, this analysis identifies reporting patterns that may warrant further review. These observations are not evidence of wrongdoing. The accounting treatment may be valid, and further corroborating information would be needed before reaching any stronger conclusion."
