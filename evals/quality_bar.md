# Quality Bar

## A Good Output

- Uses evidence from supplied documents.
- Compares multiple periods where possible.
- Triangulates income statement, balance sheet, cash flow, notes, and commentary.
- Uses calculations.
- Separates fact, interpretation, and open question.
- Includes benign explanations.
- Includes management questions.
- Avoids legal conclusions.
- Avoids investment recommendations.
- States limitations.
- Is useful to a senior finance professional.
- Contains no build-input references.

## A Bad Output

- Makes accusations.
- Implies wrongdoing.
- Relies only on headline earnings.
- Ignores cash flow.
- Ignores balance sheet.
- Ignores notes.
- Treats one signal as conclusive.
- Lacks calculations.
- Lacks uncertainty.
- Sounds sensational.
- Gives buy, sell, or hold guidance.
- Mentions or reveals non-public build inputs.
