# Sample Public Case Study

## 1. Hook

ExampleCo's quarter is a useful reminder that earnings quality depends on more than the headline growth rate.

## 2. What The Headline Numbers Say

Revenue increased 18%, and adjusted EBITDA margin expanded. Management attributed the improvement to enterprise demand and operating leverage.

## 3. What The Balance Sheet Adds

Receivables and contract assets increased 34%, faster than revenue. That does not prove a problem, but it does make cash collection and billing terms important follow-up areas.

## 4. What Cash Flow Confirms Or Challenges

Operating cash flow declined despite higher profit. The cash-flow statement indicates the main driver was working capital, especially customer balances.

## 5. What Adjusted Metrics Emphasize

The adjusted metric highlights margin expansion. The reconciliation should be reviewed for recurring exclusions and cash effects.

## 6. What The Key Red Flags Are

- Revenue growth outpaced customer-balance conversion.
- Operating cash flow did not support the profit trend this quarter.
- Disclosure did not include enough aging or collection detail to resolve the question.

## 7. What Could Be Benign

- Enterprise billing timing.
- A large customer contract.
- Seasonal collections.
- Normal expansion in longer-duration contracts.

## 8. Questions For Management

1. What portion of quarter-end receivables was collected after period-end?
2. Did payment terms change?
3. How much of the working-capital movement should reverse?

## 9. Safe Conclusion

This is a follow-up question, not an allegation. The accounting treatment may be valid, and more information would be needed before forming a stronger view.
