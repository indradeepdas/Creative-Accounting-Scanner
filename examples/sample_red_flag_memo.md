# Sample Earnings-Quality Red-Flag Memo

Company: ExampleCo
Ticker: EXM
Reporting period: Q2 2026
Documents reviewed: Q2 earnings release and quarterly financial statements
Date of analysis: 2026-06-19
Analysis mode: Quick scan

## 1. Executive Summary

Overall view: Watchlist

ExampleCo reported 18% revenue growth and improved adjusted EBITDA margin. The main follow-up area is cash support: operating cash flow declined while receivables and contract assets increased faster than revenue. The documents reviewed do not provide receivable aging or post-period collection data, so the observation should be treated as a question for follow-up rather than a conclusion.

## 2. Data Availability and Limitations

Reviewed documents include the Q2 earnings release and quarterly financial statements. Missing documents include the latest earnings call transcript, receivable aging, contract asset rollforward, and prior-quarter presentation. The analysis is limited because customer-balance detail is summarized.

## 3. Overall Scorecard

Area | Status | Severity | Evidence Strength | Confidence | Comment
--- | --- | --- | --- | --- | ---
Revenue quality | Watchlist | Medium | Moderate | 65% | Revenue growth outpaced cash support.
Customer balances | Watchlist | Medium | Moderate | 65% | Receivables and contract assets grew faster than revenue.
Cash-flow quality | Watchlist | Medium | Moderate | 60% | Operating cash flow declined despite profit growth.
Adjusted metrics | Clean | Low | Weak | 45% | Reconciliation is provided, but history is limited.

## 4. Top Red Flags

- Observation: Receivables and contract assets increased faster than revenue.
- Evidence: Revenue grew 18%; combined customer balances grew 34%.
- Calculation: 34% customer-balance growth minus 18% revenue growth = 16 percentage-point gap.
- Why it matters: Revenue growth is stronger when supported by collection or deferred revenue evidence.
- Possible benign explanations: Enterprise billing timing, large customer contract, seasonal collections.
- Follow-up question: What collections occurred after quarter-end and what changed in payment terms?
- Severity: Medium.
- Evidence strength: Moderate.
- Confidence: 65%.

## 5. Income Statement vs Balance Sheet Bridge

The income statement shows growth and margin improvement. The balance sheet adds caution because customer balances expanded faster than revenue and the filing does not provide enough detail to distinguish normal billing timing from slower conversion.

## 6. Profit vs Cash Bridge

Profit improved while operating cash flow declined. The cash-flow statement indicates working capital was the main driver, especially receivables and contract assets.

## 7. Adjusted Metrics Review

Adjusted EBITDA is reconciled to GAAP operating income. The largest exclusions appear to be acquisition-related costs and stock-based compensation. More history is needed to determine recurrence.

## 8. Management Questions

1. What explains the difference between revenue growth and customer-balance growth?
2. What percentage of receivables was collected after quarter-end?
3. Did payment terms change for large customers?
4. How much revenue came from new contracts versus renewals?
5. Which adjusted EBITDA exclusions are expected to recur?

## 9. What Would Reduce Concern

- Receivable aging showing stable or improved collection profile.
- Post-quarter cash collection data.
- Contract asset rollforward.
- Clear explanation of large customer billing timing.

## 10. Documents Needed for Further Review

- Earnings call transcript.
- Prior-quarter presentation.
- Receivable aging or collection commentary.
- Contract asset rollforward.

## 11. Legal-Safe Conclusion

"Based on the documents reviewed, this analysis identifies reporting patterns that may warrant further review. These observations are not evidence of wrongdoing. The accounting treatment may be valid, and further corroborating information would be needed before reaching any stronger conclusion."
