# Public Case Study

## 1. Hook

Open with a calm, factual reason the quarter is worth studying.

## 2. What The Headline Numbers Say

Summarize revenue, profit, margin, and management's main message.

## 3. What The Balance Sheet Adds

Discuss receivables, contract balances, inventory, accrued expenses, debt, and other relevant balances.

## 4. What Cash Flow Confirms Or Challenges

Compare profit with operating cash flow and free cash flow. Explain working-capital effects.

## 5. What Adjusted Metrics Emphasize

Describe adjusted metrics, reconciliations, recurring exclusions, and cash effects.

## 6. What The Key Red Flags Are

List evidence-backed patterns that may warrant further review.

## 7. What Could Be Benign

List plausible valid explanations.

## 8. Questions For Management

Ask sharp, respectful questions.

## 9. Safe Conclusion

End without allegations, legal conclusions, or investment recommendations.

Tone requirements:

- Calm.
- Forensic.
- Precise.
- Non-accusatory.
- Useful to finance professionals.
- No outrage.
- No stock recommendation.
